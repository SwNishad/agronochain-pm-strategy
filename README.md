# agronochain-pm-strategy
A Product Management case study: Engineering a "phygital" onboarding strategy to scale Agronochain to 100,000 farmers by 2026.


# Scaling Agronochain: The Amanot 2.0 Strategy 🌾
**Transforming Onboarding from a Manual Bottleneck to a Viral Growth Engine.**

## 🚀 Company Overview & The Mandate
Agronochain is a revolutionary agritech platform optimizing Bangladesh's agricultural supply chain by directly connecting farmers, traders, and institutional buyers. They combat the country's prevalent 40% post-harvest crop loss by providing climate-resilient drying and storage facilities, alongside fair pricing and instant liquidity. 

**The Mandate:** Scale the network from the current baseline of 20,000 farmers to 100,000 connected farmers, establish 50 warehouses, and transact 1 million tonnes of harvest by 2026.

## 🔍 Deep Forensic Research
To engineer a superior acquisition engine, I conducted an extensive research phase:
* **Macro Data:** Analyzed rural infrastructure and digital literacy data via the Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics (BBS).
* **Industry Reports:** Quantified post-harvest loss and market dynamics using data from LightCastle Partners and the World Bank.
* **Local Competitor Intelligence:** Conducted tear-downs of local incumbents like iFarmer, Fashol, and AgroShift to analyze their onboarding and logistics models.
* **Global Competitor Intelligence:** Examined the existential scaling dangers experienced by international players like WayCool (India) and Twiga Foods (Kenya) to validate the need for an asset-light model.

## 🚨 The Core Challenge & Friction Points
**The Problem:** The current manual, agent-heavy onboarding process is unscalable and expensive, leading to lost farmers due to post-harvest decay and middleman exploitation. 

I mapped out the current onboarding insights and farmer pain points into an affinity diagram, identifying key friction points:
* **"The Decay Anxiety":** Crops rot faster than digital signups happen.
* **"Cash-Flow Fear":** Middlemen win because apps take 1 to 3 days to settle payments.
* **"Technostress":** Illiterate farmers freeze when faced with text menus.
* **"The Black Box Effect":** Rural trust requires physical tangibility, not just invisible SMS balances.

## 👥 The Dual Persona Strategy
Because the primary user is technologically averse, I created a dual persona strategy:
* **The Vulnerable Farmer (Rahim Akbar):** A 52-year-old illiterate potato farmer using a feature phone, driven by immediate cash-flow needs.
* **The Digital Catalyst (Md Sumon):** A 22-year-old smartphone-savvy university student who acts as the "Community Validator" to bridge the digital gap.

## 🗺️ Current vs. Ideal User Journey
I created a visual User Journey Map highlighting the drastic emotional shifts between the current corporate-led flow and the ideal community-led flow:
* **Current Journey:** Farmers experience 😟 **Suspicion** during awareness, 😨 **Fear** during onboarding, 😟 **Anxiety** during the transaction, and 😫 **Frustration** waiting for payments.
* **Ideal Journey:** By leveraging the Agri-Ambassador and instant liquidity, reactions shift to 😲 **Curiosity**, 😌 **Relief**, 🤠 **Trust**, and 😍 **Delight**.

## 💡 Ideation & RICE Prioritization
I brainstormed multiple strategic interventions to solve these pain points. To balance engineering effort against the goal of 100,000 farmers, I utilized the **RICE Framework** (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) to prioritize the top features:
1. **Express Drop-Off:** Weigh first, register second (Score: 202).
2. **"Agri-Ambassador" Mode:** Crowdsourced onboarding via local youth (Score: 182).
3. **Instant Partial-Pay:** Immediate 20% cash disbursement via API (Score: 115).

## 📱 Prototyping: From Lo-Fi to Hi-Fi
To operationalize the "Agri-Ambassador" app feature:
* **Low-Fidelity Wireframes (Figma):** Mapped the core information architecture, utilizing massive icon-led tap targets to eliminate reading reliance and a camera-first "Snap-to-Apply" registration flow.
* **High-Fidelity Prototype (Lovable):** Built a functional, interactive UI that brings the localized Amanot Hub and gamified Ambassador dashboard to life.

## 📈 Success Metrics (Business Outcomes)
To track real business outcomes rather than vanity metrics, I established:
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Target to drop CAC by 50%+ via the Ambassador gig-model.
* **Time-to-Value (TTV):** Reduce drop-off times from 1-3 days to under 30 minutes.
* **Activation Rate:** Target >80% to ensure registered users actually deposit physical crops.

## ⚙️ Agile Execution: Jira & Phased Roadmap
* **Jira Backlog:** Translated the strategy into sprint-ready epics and user stories, utilizing the MoSCoW prioritization framework (labeling core tasks as "Must-Have").
* **Phased Roadmap:** Developed a 5-month roadmap (March to July 2026) that de-risks the launch by securing physical warehouse operations (Offline-First ERPs) before scaling the digital growth engine.
